Help me Get Registered to Vote

Option 1: Online Registration

We strongly encourage everyone to register to vote online if they can, as it will be easier to vote on election day if you do so. The deadline for online registration is October 13th.

Clarification: "Checking" vs. "Updating" vs. "Registering"

These core concepts are poorly explained on the Elections Canada website. Let's examine each in turn:

  • Checking your Registration: If you voted in the last election, reside at the same address and haven't changed your name, then you're probably fine, and the service will simply confirm that you are registered to vote.
  • Updating your Registration: In the case that you've moved, you should enter the address that you had when you last voted, not your current address. This will kickstart the registration update process. This requires less identification than the full-blown registration process. If you've changed your name, you also will need to update, but that will require a paper form - contact your local Elections Canda Office for more information.
  • Registering: If you have never voted before, you will have to register. This will require some extra information - in every province except Quebec, your driver's license will suffice. In Alberta, Newfoundland and Labrador, Saskatchewan and Yukon, your provincial ID will also work. If none of these options are available for you, you'll have to register using a method other than online registration.

Conditions That Could Stop You From Registering Online

  • You've changed your name since you last voted.
  • You live abroad.
  • You're a member of the Canadian Forces.
  • You don't have the proper address type. It needs to be one of these three: civic (for example, 123 Main Street), lot and concession, or Section-Township-Range-Meridian.
  • You don't have proper identification. This one is tricky, and deserves some attention:
    • In every province except for Quebec, your provincial driver's license is an acceptable form of identification

If you're good on all of these fronts, then you should proceed to the registration page. Armed with this knowledge, the steps should be fairly self-explanatory.
