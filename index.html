Think you're ready to vote in the 2015 Canadian Election?

You might not be. The requirements have changed.

Don't worry, we've got your back. We'll help you get registered to vote in the 2015 Election.



Hurry! Online voter registration closes in .

The biggest change: you'll need an ID with your current address.

Voter information cards don't count.

Unlike last time, you can't use one of these to prove your address.

Driver's Licenses and IDs do, but...

If you move often, these probably don't contain your current address.

Thankfully, There are alternatives...

These include your lease, electricity / telecomm bills, and bank statements .

This is just the tip of the iceberg.

Did you know that:

  • The geographical layouts of the ridings have changed.
  • Elections Canada is limited in its ability to promote voting.
  • You can no longer vouch for multiple people's identities.
The list goes on...

We're looking into these issues so you don't have to.

We won't spam you.
We're here to help.

“It is essential to understand that the main challenge for our electoral democracy is not voter fraud, but voter participation. I do not believe that if we eliminate vouching and the [information card] as a proof of address, we will have in any way improved the integrity of the voting process. However, we will have taken away the ability of many qualified electors to vote.”

- Marc Mayrand, Chief Electoral Officer of Elections Canada
