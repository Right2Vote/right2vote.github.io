The biggest change: you'll need an ID with your current address.

Voter information cards don't count.

Unlike last time, you can't use one of these to prove your address.

Driver's Licenses and IDs do, but...

If you move often, these probably don't contain your current address.

Thankfully, There are alternatives...

These include your lease, electricity / phone bills, and bank statements.

“Based on the evidence to date, there is a risk that some individuals who would otherwise rely on the Voter Information Card to enable them to vote will be unable to do so due to s.46(3), which would result in irreparable harm due to their inability to exercise their right to vote in that fashion.”

- Justice David Stinson (emphasis ours)
